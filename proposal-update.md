## Job Role In Detail
The central goal of this role is to turn the Winteriors website into an active sales channel. Everything I do will work towards that goal. Here is how the work flows in practice.

### The Foundation: A Well-Maintained Website
Before anything else, the website needs to work properly. This means accurate information, working links, up-to-date content, and a functional shop page. Think of the website as Winteriors' virtual store. Just like the physical store, it should reflect what is actually available. If a sofa is shown on the website, it should be available at the store. Prices should match. That way a customer's expectations are met when they walk through the door.

### Collaborating with the In-Store Sales Team
A big part of this role involves staying closely connected with the in-store team. I will need to know what products are available, what promotions are running, and when new stork arrives. To keep things simple, I propose a brief weekly check-in. This could be a short WhatsApp message, a phone call, or whatever works best for the team. The goal is to make sure that what is on the website always reflects what is happening in the store. Over time, I hope the team will become familiar with the website so that they can refer customers to it when needed.

### The Core Cycle: Product Promotion
Once the website is in order, the work settles into a repeating cycle centred on products. For each product I promote, the process looks like this:

1. Profile the product. Give it a name, description, price, images, dimensions, materials, available colours, and so on. Publish the product on the website with a clear Call To Action (CTA) encouraging visitors to enquire or place an order.
2. Write a detailed blog article about the product. The article gives potential customers the information they need to make a purchase decision. It also forms the basis of every other piece of content that follows, making the rest of the work more efficient.
3. Using the article as source material, create a post to share across relevant online platforms and forums, with links back to the product page on the website. I will monitor and respond to any interest this generates.
4. At the end of the week, send a summary of the week's featured products to the mailing list and the WhatsApp group. One update each, keeping subscribers informed without overwhelming them.

This cycle keeps the website fresh, feeds the blog, drives traffic from online platforms, and keeps the mailing list and WhatsApp group active. All from one piece of work.

### Building an Audience: Mailing List and WhatsApp Group
To make the weekly updates possible, I will set up two opt-in channels on the website. The first is a mailing list sign-up form for people who prefer WhatsApp. Both server the same purpose, keeping interested customers informed about new products, promotions, and updates from Winteriors. Over time, these two channels become a growing list of warm leads that the business can communicate with directly.

### Managing Enquiries and Leads
As interest comes in through the contact form, social posts, or direct messages, I will log all potential leads and follow up appropriately. In the Zambian context, WhatsApp and email tend to be the most effective ways to communicate with customers, so those will be the primary channels for follow-up.

### Search Engine Optimisation(SEO)
SEO is the practice of making a website easier to find on search engines like Google. When someone searches for "sofa in Lusaka" or "furniture store in Zambia", the goal is for the Winteriors website to appear among the top results. The higher a website ranks, the more likely a potential customer is to click on it. SEO is not a one-time task, it is something that improves gradually over time. The blog articles contribute to it naturally by adding relevant content on a regular basis. Beyond that, I will periodically review how the website is performing in search results and make adjustments as needed.

### Reporting
I will keep an ongoing log of what is working and what needs adjustment. At the end of each week I will share a brief update with the Winteriors team covering what was published, what engagement was received, and any leads that came in. This keeps everyone informed and ensures the work remains aligned with the business's goals.

### Expanding the Scope Over Time
As the core cycle becomes more efficient, I will look to introduce additional features to the website. One example is a Cost Estimator, a tool that allows visitors to get a rough price estimate for the furniture they are interested in before visiting the store. This can help customers plan their budget and increases the likelihood of a purchase. These additions will be introduced gradually as the foundation grows stronger.

## Week One Plan
If you offer me this role, here is what I will accomplish in the first week.

### Days 1 - 2: Website Update
I will apply all the fixes outlined in the Winteriors Website Improvement Plan, ensuring the website is clean, all links work, and the information is accurate and up to date.

### Days 3 - 4: Product Listings
I will work with the in-store sales team to select 6 products to feature on the website. I will profile each product with a name, description, price, image and additional details such as dimensions, materials and available colours. Each product will have its own page with a clear Call To Action (CTA) encouraging visitors to place an order.

### Day 5 - 6: Launch and First Promotion
With the products live on the website, I will share the product pages across relevant online platforms and forums. Posts will include product details and links designed to capture potential leads. I will monitor and respond to any interest generated.

### Ongoing from Day 1: Activity Log
Throughout the week I will keep a log of what is working and what needs adjustment. This will form the basis of a brief end-of-week update I will share with the Winteriors team.
